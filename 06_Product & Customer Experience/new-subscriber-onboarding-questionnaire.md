# BEHIND THE SCENES: Why These Questions Work

## Psychological Design Principles

### 1. **Start with Victory (Questions 3-4)**
- Asking about "best recent projects" triggers positive memories
- People LOVE talking about their wins
- Creates dopamine hit early in questionnaire (increases completion rate)
- Frames the questionnaire as "helping you get more of what you love" not "interrogation"

### 2. **Make It Feel Like Bragging, Not Reporting**
- "Your crown jewel" vs "Project #1"
- "Who do you love working with?" vs "List your clients"
- "Your dream project" vs "Ideal project parameters"
- Language mattersâ€”make them feel like a badass, not a data entry clerk

### 3. **Memory-Based Questions Only**
- No one needs to look anything up
- No "what was your revenue in 2023?" questions
- Everything is answerable from memory in the moment
- Reduces friction, increases completion

### 4. **Progressive Disclosure**
- Start easy (name, company)
- Build to more thoughtful (dream project, vision)
- End easy (communication preferences)
- Avoids overwhelming them upfront

### 5. **Give Examples and Context**
- "Example: 180-unit apartment complex"
- Shows them what kind of answer we're looking for
- Reduces cognitive load

### 6. **Make It About THEM, Not Us**
- "Let's find you MORE jobs like your best ones"
- "Where are you taking this business?"
- "What would make this a home run for YOU?"
- Every section is framed as being FOR their benefit

### 7. **Show Immediate Value**
- Explain WHY we're asking each question
- "ðŸ’¡ Why we ask: When we see these names in planning agendas, you'll get instant alerts"
- They understand the value exchange

### 8. **Create Anticipation**
- "You're about to gain an unfair advantage"
- "Think of these questions as a chance to brag"
- "Welcome to your unfair advantage"
- Excitement-building language throughout

### 9. **Remove Decision Fatigue**
- Multiple choice whenever possible
- Checkboxes, not free-form text (except where stories matter)
- Pre-populated ranges for $ values
- Makes it FAST

### 10. **Vision Questions Build Emotional Investment**
- "Your dream project"
- "Where are you taking this business?"
- When they articulate their vision, they mentally commit to using the tool to achieve it

## Technical Implementation Notes

### Data We're Actually Collecting:

**For Personalization:**
- Company name, type, capabilities (feeds into fit scoring)
- Past successful projects (teaches AI what "good" looks like)
- Preferred developers/engineers (instant alert triggers)
- Ideal project size, location, capacity (filters universe)

**For AI Training:**
- What made projects "great" (teaches AI what to prioritize)
- Dream project description (reveals unspoken preferences)
- Growth goals (helps us understand risk tolerance, ambition level)
- Success metrics (what they're optimizing for)

**For Delivery:**
- Team members and roles (who gets what alerts)
- Communication preferences (text/email, frequency, threshold)
- Contact info (obvious but critical)

**For Engagement:**
- How they heard about us (tracks marketing effectiveness)
- What would make this a "home run" (success criteria we can measure against)

### Completion Rate Optimization:

**Target: 85%+ completion rate**

**Tactics:**
1. **Progress bar at top** (shows "62% complete" to encourage finishing)
2. **Auto-save** (if they abandon, we can email "finish your setup in 30 seconds")

4. **Skip options** (for team members, preferencesâ€”nothing is truly "required" except basics)
5. **Estimated time shown** (3 minutesâ€”sets expectation, creates commitment)
